#  ESG Risk Register Guide  
### *How to identify, assess, and manage ESG risks effectively*

An ESG risk register helps organizations track **environmental, social, and governance risks**, assign accountability, and plan mitigation.

It is essential for:
- CSRD compliance  
- Sustainable business operations  
- Risk-based ESG reporting  
- Board oversight  
- Supply-chain monitoring  

---

# 1 Categories of ESG Risks

## **Environmental Risks**
- Climate change impacts (floods, heatwaves)
- Carbon regulation penalties
- Waste & pollution liabilities
- Water scarcity
- Biodiversity loss

## **Social Risks**
- Labor rights violations
- Workplace health & safety incidents
- Diversity and inclusion gaps
- Supply-chain exploitation risks
- Community conflicts

## **Governance Risks**
- Corruption & bribery
- Weak internal controls
- Lack of ESG oversight
- Inaccurate sustainability reporting
- Compliance failures (CSRD, sanctions)

---

# 2 How to Build an ESG Risk Register

## **Step 1 — Identify ESG Risks**
Sources:
- Operations  
- Supply chain  
- Regulations  
- Stakeholders  
- Internal audits  
- Materiality assessment  

## **Step 2 — Assess Risks**
Rate each on:
- Impact (1–5)  
- Likelihood (1–5)  
- Velocity (how fast it materializes)  

## **Step 3 — Assign Ownership**
Departments:
- Sustainability  
- Operations  
- HR  
- Compliance  
- Supply chain  
- Finance  

## **Step 4 — Define Controls & Mitigation**
Examples:
- Carbon reduction plan  
- Anti-corruption training  
- Supplier audits  
- Waste management program  

## **Step 5 — Monitor & Update Quarterly**
Sustainability risks evolve faster than typical enterprise risks.

# 3 ESG Risk Scoring Matrix

| Score | Impact Level | Description |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| 1–5 | Low | Minor or limited effect |
| 6–10 | Medium | Noticeable disruption |
| 11–15 | High | Major operational risk |
| 16–25 | Critical | Severe financial or reputational damage |

---

#  Key Data to Capture in the Register

- Risk title  
- Category (E/S/G)  
- Description  
- Impact score  
- Likelihood score  
- Velocity  
- Inherent risk rating  
- Mitigation controls  
- Residual risk rating  
- Risk owner  
- Review frequency  

---

# ✔️ Summary  
An ESG risk register is a **mandatory tool** for sustainable operations, audit readiness, and CSRD-aligned reporting. It ensures visibility, governance, and proactive risk management.
